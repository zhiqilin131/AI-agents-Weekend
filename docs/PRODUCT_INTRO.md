# Foresight-X

*A companion for decisions that matter.*

---

## Why it exists

Big choices rarely come with a neat pros-and-cons list. You carry your history, your doubts, and a few things you already know about yourself—but it is easy to forget them in the moment, or to talk in circles.

**Foresight-X** is built for that gap. You describe your situation in your own words. The product helps you **see real options**, **surface what you already care about**, and **leave a record** you can return to—not a single clever answer dropped into a chat box, but a structured path from question to reflection.

---

## What you get

**Clarity without pretending to live your life.**  
You stay in charge. The product suggests angles, flags common blind spots, and sketches possible futures as **food for thought**, not fortune-telling.

**Continuity over time.**  
What you save—your priorities, notes from reflection, and past decision summaries—can inform the next question. The idea is simple: **fewer “I forgot what mattered to me” moments.**

**Two complementary spaces**

- **Decisions (home)** — For “what should I do?” You get options, reasoning, a gentle look at risks and biases, and a written report you can keep.

- **Shadow** — For “what am I really feeling?” A private, reflective conversation—closer to **thinking alongside yourself** than to a help desk. Things you choose to keep can flow into your profile so decision mode can use them later.

---

## How a decision session feels

You write what is going on—honestly, messily, in plain language. If something important is missing, you may see **a few short questions** to sharpen the picture (not a long form).

From there, Foresight-X weaves in **what you have told it before** (priorities, constraints, values, and facts you have allowed it to remember) and, when helpful, **similar threads from your own past decisions** and **public information** (if that feature is turned on where you use it).

You receive a report you can read at your own pace: recommended direction, other paths, evidence and memory in one place, **simulated futures** as imagination aids—not predictions—and space to reflect. If you want to go deeper on **one specific option**, **Option Coach** lets you keep asking “how would I actually do this?” in a focused thread that remembers that option’s conversation.

Later, you can **note what really happened** and how you feel about it, so your future self can compare **expectation and reality**. You can also skim **past decisions** whenever you need grounding.

---

## Your profile: the story of “you” the product remembers

Think of the profile as **the facts and lines you are willing to stand behind**—not everything the system ever heard.

- **Your priorities** — What you type here stays yours; it is treated as your voice first.

- **From short questionnaires** — Answers from those pre-analysis check-ins, kept in one place; you can remove what no longer fits.

- **Structured memory** — Concrete details (who you are, what you prefer, how you tend to act), often coming from Shadow or things you said; you can edit or delete.

- **About you, constraints, values** — Room for narrative and bullet points that shape how advice is framed.

The product is designed so **what you write on purpose** leads, and remembered details support—not replace—that.

---

## Words you might see on screen (in everyday language)

- **Past decisions / patterns** — Drawn from **reports you chose to save**, not from dumping entire chat logs. The more you save thoughtful runs, the richer this becomes.

- **World facts / references** — Information about the outside world (sometimes including the open web). It can be incomplete or dated; use it as one input among many.

- **Recent notes** — A blend of profile snippets, reflection notes, and past decision summaries, with an emphasis on what seems **relevant to the question you asked now**. If you are new or rarely save anything, some areas may feel quiet—that is natural.

---

## Privacy, in plain terms

In typical personal use, your profile and reports stay **on the device or setup where you run the app**; nothing ships to the cloud by itself. If your organization hosts Foresight-X on a server, your admin can explain where data lives.

---

## Important boundaries

Foresight-X is **not therapy** and **not for emergencies**. If you are in crisis, please reach out to local emergency services or a trusted professional.

The product **does not decide for you**. Simulated futures are **illustrations** to think with. Any automated text can be wrong—check what matters against real life and people you trust.
